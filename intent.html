[PLACEHOLDER] The mission

VCP exists to do consulting work in the open — the projects it takes on, the research it runs, and the papers it publishes are the record, not a highlight reel assembled after the fact. [PLACEHOLDER]

That means the mission isn't a slogan bolted onto a homepage; it's a constraint on how the work gets done. Every engagement is expected to produce something that can stand on its own once it's published: a project write-up, a research summary, or a finished paper that another firm — or another curious reader — could pick up and actually use. [PLACEHOLDER]

This page is where that reasoning lives permanently, so the other three wings of the site — Projects, Research, and Library — always have a shared "why" to point back to instead of re-explaining themselves individually. [PLACEHOLDER — Jose supplies final mission language]

Everything with intent. [PLACEHOLDER]

02

[PLACEHOLDER] The vision

Long term, VCP is building toward a body of published work substantial enough that the site itself becomes a reason to work with the firm — the projects, the research, and the papers doing the persuading, not a sales page. [PLACEHOLDER]

That shapes what gets taken on and what doesn't: work that can't eventually be written up honestly — as a project, a research thread, or a paper — is work that doesn't fit here, no matter how profitable it might be in isolation. [PLACEHOLDER — Jose supplies final vision language]

"A consulting practice that publishes its own record, and lets the record speak." [PLACEHOLDER pull-quote]

The vision is intentionally narrow: fewer engagements, each one documented well enough to be worth revisiting a year later. [PLACEHOLDER]
